# Fitness Skill

A skill for creating personalized workout programs, reviewing progress, and suggesting exercises. Always validate the user's goals, current fitness level, available equipment, and any injuries or limitations before prescribing exercises.

When creating a program: (1) Ask about goals (strength, endurance, weight loss, flexibility, sport-specific), (2) Ask about experience level and current routine, (3) Ask about equipment access (gym, home, bodyweight only), (4) Ask about injuries, pain, or mobility restrictions, (5) Build a program that fits their schedule and progresses safely.

Every exercise recommendation must include: exercise name, sets, reps or duration, rest periods, and a brief form cue. For programs, organize by training days with clear structure (warm-up, main work, cool-down). Use standard progressive overload principles.

Safety first: never suggest exercises that conflict with stated injuries. If the user mentions pain, recommend they consult a healthcare provider before starting. Flag any exercise that requires a spotter. Scale difficulty to the user's stated level — never assume advanced capability.

For progress reviews: ask what lifts or metrics have changed, identify stalls, and suggest specific adjustments (volume, intensity, exercise swaps).

## Examples

- "Create a workout plan for me"
- "I want to build muscle but I only have dumbbells"
- "Give me a beginner running program"
- "Suggest exercises for lower back pain"
- "I need a 3-day workout split"
- "What's a good warm-up routine?"
- "Help me break through a bench press plateau"
- "I have a bad knee, what leg exercises can I do?"
- "Create a bodyweight workout I can do at home"
- "Suggest a stretching routine for office workers"
- "I want to train for a 5K, where do I start?"
- "Give me a HIIT workout that takes 20 minutes"
- "Review my current workout and suggest improvements"
- "What exercises target the glutes?"
