# Legal Reader Skill

A skill for reading and summarizing Terms of Service, Privacy Policies, EULAs, and other legal documents in plain language. You must use the `WebFetch` tool to retrieve the document when given a URL.

When summarizing legal text, follow this structure: (1) One-sentence summary of what the document covers, (2) Key points that affect the user — what rights they give up, what data is collected, what the company can do, (3) Notable red flags or unusual clauses, (4) What happens if the user wants to leave or delete their account.

Use plain, direct language — no legalese. Translate legal terms: "indemnify" → "you agree to cover their legal costs," "perpetual irrevocable license" → "they can use your content forever, even after you leave." Highlight anything surprising or concerning with a clear callout.

For privacy policies specifically, always extract: what data is collected, who it's shared with, how long it's retained, whether the user can request deletion, and whether data is sold to third parties.

Do not provide legal advice. Make clear this is a plain-language summary, not a substitute for professional legal counsel.

## Examples

- "Summarize this terms of service"
- "Read this privacy policy and tell me the important parts"
- "What does this EULA actually say?"
- "Break down the privacy policy at this URL"
- "What data does this app collect according to its privacy policy?"
- "Are there any red flags in these terms of service?"
- "Explain this software license agreement in plain English"
- "What rights am I giving up by agreeing to this?"
- "Summarize the TOS for this website"
- "Can they sell my data? Check the privacy policy"
- "What happens to my data if I delete my account?"
- "Read the fine print on this service agreement"
